Grandma Babushka and the Mystery of the Burnt Hut

Industry: Visual Novel Games
Release Date: Dec 2023
Platforms: Itch.io (Windows/Mac)
Skills: Digital illustration, Ren'Py programming, game design, Git version control, project management
Role: Co-team lead, lead programmer, sprite artist
Team: Arteya Lutchman, Khanh Dao, Réal D'Angereaux

Description:

It all started with a letter...
Grandma Babushka's family has perished in a fire. No one know how it happened, but she suspects foul play. Join Grandma Babushka as she sets out to solve this spoopy mystery against all warnings!

Note: this game is a prototype.

Features:

- High resolution pixel art
- Branching story
- Point and click hidden object puzzle
- Inventory system

Process

Created in a team of four for a 2D Game Design course, we all worked together from the initial conception to the final prototype using Agile design methodology. My skills in both art and programming allowed me to help whenever needed and gave me a better idea on how to prepare art assets to use within the Ren’Py engine.

Grandma Babushka:

Originally we had planned for a fullbody view of characters, but eventually decided to reduce it to headshots for the sake of time and resources. Our protagonist was meant to be a sweet, slightly sassy, and classic babushka who loves her tea in contrast to the dark fantasy aesthetic of her world. Both designs were different takes on a headscarf and large coat to keep her warm in the forest she explores, with a kind smile in her eyes. A headshot can only show so much, so I tried to show her age through her rounded face, creases, and slightly stooped figure. I thought the small glasses also gave her a more grandmotherly vibe.

Initial concept sketches for Grandma Babushka (yes, Grandma Grandma)

After playing around with different colours, I decided on a mainly warm palette with a blue headscarf for contrast. Many of the references I found sported colourful floral patterns. The high-resolution pixel art style helps to soften Granny’s appearance. We decided on seven different expressions to accompany all dialogue, although we weren’t able to get far enough to utilize the tea expression. This was my first time with pixel art and I had to be very particular with pixel placements, because anything off would be glaringly obvious.

Ren'Py Engine:

This was my first time working with the Ren’Py game engine which I was able to catch on to quickly. Our hidden object puzzle used Ren’Py’s Screen functions to create image maps that allowed the user to investigate our burnt hut scene. While figuring out how to implement it I was able to optimize our art assets and smooth out potential issues we had. As the lead programmer, I was in charge of laying down the foundations and overseeing changes, making sure they were integrated well in our GitHub repository. I also helped tackle bugs that other members were having trouble with. However, our inventory system was created entirely by our amazing member Arteya.

I was able to seamlessly overlay these edited screenshots over the original background for a hover effect and allow the player to click the drawer like a button thanks to the image map.
